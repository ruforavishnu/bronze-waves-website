BLOCK FACTORY

Automatic Block Factory was established in 2014 by BRONZE WAVES GROUP is one of the leading block manufacturers in Sohar today. For the past 2 years we achieved a good name in the local market by strictly following up the standard as per the Oman Municipality rules and regulations. The quality production and efficient service help us to obtain the approval certificate from Oman Development Authority for specified projects.

Concrete blocks have become increasingly popular in the gulf area, mainly because of their inherent strength, economy and resistance to fire, but their excellent insulation qualities make them ideal for hot climates. Modern times create the need for modern designs. That is why a variety of concrete blocks with various textures and shapes are produced to satisfy the needs at costs substantially lower than similiar materials.

FACILITIES/INFRASTRUCTURE

Our infrastructure consists of fully automated plants and machineries from Germany and Italy which can produces 10000 blocks per day. A large fleet of trucks is available for transport of finished goods to the customers site. Adequate materials handling equipment in terms of forklifts, cranes and shovels are also available. In house garage is available for trucks servicing and repairs, a well-equipped workshop is also available for house fabrication and maintenance of machines.

PRODUCTION PROCESS

The factory comprises of a batching plant having a capacity of 1.5 cubic meter. The mix is taken by means of computer controlled equipments and has the facility to deliver the exact quantity for each batch required for the manufacturing process. No manual operation is involved in the batching stage and the respective operator montiors each mix that falls into the mixer. The raw materials like sand and aggregate flows through a rubber conveyor to the weighing scale and finally reaches into the mixer by a skip hoist system. Cement and water from respective tanks loaded into the pan via screw conveyor. After enough mixing and compaction the wet mix transferred to the machine through a dumper. The machines run for 9 hours each day and 1 hour for cleaning and maintenance.
